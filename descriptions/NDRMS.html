User Interface Design

The Natural Disaster Relief Managment System (NDRMS) is an interface that provides local government the ability to swiftly alert citizens of imminent natural disasters.

My undergraduate student project of three focused soley on the Graphical User Interface design, this program has no backend besides text file parsers for dropdown boxes and remembering past broadcasts to recall them every time the program is opened. In this GUI you can see current broadcasts and past broadcasts. If the user is an organizer or staff of a natural disaster relief team they can broadcast disaster warnings and disaster watches to the public. The user can also dispatch an EMS, the fire department, and law enforcement as needed to people who need it.

This GUI was insprired by psychological studies that have proven to increase usability. Its consistency in button and menu placement allows for recognition, instead of the slower method of recall. After training, the user can master completing tasks as shown in our usability tests with a sample group of 4 other students. This GUI was made in JavaFX for the graduate level Human-Computer Interface class.
